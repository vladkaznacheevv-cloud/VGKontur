# Stage 3.5 — Design Research & 2026 Web Trends

Project: Construction and renovation services in KMV  
Primary launch geo: Essentuki  
Site style: light tones, trustworthy, local, conversion-focused  
Goal of this stage: create and approve visual mockup directions before production MVP.

## Strategic design conclusion

The site should not look like a cheap “brigade landing page”.
It should feel like a reliable local construction/renovation operator:

- clean light visual system;
- clear service blocks;
- strong first-screen CTA;
- real-world construction/renovation trust cues;
- local geo emphasis: Essentuki first, KMV as wider service area;
- fast mobile-first layout;
- calculator and lead form as conversion tools;
- no excessive 3D/WebGL or heavy visual effects on MVP.

## 2026 design signals relevant to this project

### Mobile-first, performance and accessibility are baseline

For local service traffic, the site must be designed mobile-first.
The primary CTAs should be visible and reachable on mobile: call, WhatsApp, Telegram, request measurement, calculate estimate.

Performance is part of design. Heavy hero videos, excessive animations and large unoptimized image sets should be avoided for the first MVP.

### Light minimalism with human texture

The project requested light tones. The best direction is not sterile white, but warm construction minimalism:

- off-white background;
- stone / plaster / concrete texture;
- warm beige and sand tones;
- graphite text;
- muted green or terracotta accent;
- clean technical lines.

This connects “construction craft” with modern UI.

### Typography as structure

2026 award-style sites increasingly use typography as the visual architecture.
For us this means large confident hero headline, short semantic headings, clear section rhythm, strong service names and no weak generic slogans.

### Soft brutalism / engineered grid

We can borrow precise geometry, visible containers, thin lines and structured grids, but in a softer light palette.

Good for services grid, calculator panel, trust metrics and process timeline.

Avoid harsh black-white brutalism, too experimental navigation and confusing anti-grid layouts on mobile.

### Subtle motion only where it helps conversion

Recommended motion:
- button hover;
- service cards reveal;
- calculator step transitions;
- process timeline movement;
- map/geo pin micro-interaction.

Avoid long preloaders, heavy scroll hijacking, WebGL-first hero and animations that delay LCP.

## What to borrow from award websites

Use:
- architecture-like grid discipline;
- large airy spacing;
- minimal navigation;
- editorial hero composition;
- high-quality renovation/interior visuals;
- subtle texture;
- smooth micro-interactions;
- strong responsive mobile layout.

Do not use:
- slow artistic intros;
- confusing navigation;
- ultra-abstract copy;
- hidden CTAs;
- cinematic animations that do not produce leads.

## MVP design principle

> Award-level aesthetics, local-service conversion logic.
