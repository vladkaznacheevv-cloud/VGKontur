# Stage 3.5 — Design Approval & QA Criteria

Use this checklist to approve a mockup before Stage 4 development.

## 1. Conversion criteria

The mockup passes if:
- first screen explains the offer in 3 seconds;
- primary CTA is visible without scrolling on mobile;
- phone / WhatsApp / Telegram are easy to reach;
- free measurement and estimate are visible;
- trust points are clear;
- services are understandable;
- calculator does not distract from the main CTA.

## 2. Mobile criteria

The mockup passes if:
- mobile layout feels native, not squeezed desktop;
- buttons are large enough;
- text is readable;
- no overloaded hero;
- no wide tables;
- process section is easy to scan;
- form is simple.

## 3. Visual criteria

The mockup passes if:
- light tone is maintained;
- design feels trustworthy and professional;
- no cheap template feeling;
- no sterile empty white page;
- textures are subtle;
- visuals support construction/renovation reality;
- no fake awards, fake team photos or unrealistic claims.

## 4. Technical feasibility

The mockup passes if:
- can be built as a Next.js landing page;
- no heavy mandatory WebGL;
- no long preloader;
- no animation blocking LCP;
- images can be optimized to WebP/AVIF;
- layout is compatible with mobile-first implementation.

## 5. Content criteria

The mockup passes if it includes:
- repair and finishing works in Essentuki;
- construction and renovation across KMV;
- free measurement;
- estimate;
- quality control;
- specialists 10+ years;
- services list;
- process;
- calculator;
- lead form;
- contacts;
- service area.

## 6. Approval decision format

For each generated mockup, evaluate:
- Visual trust: /10
- Conversion clarity: /10
- Mobile readiness: /10
- Technical simplicity: /10
- Brand potential: /10

Decision:
- approve as primary;
- approve with changes;
- reject;
- combine with another direction.
